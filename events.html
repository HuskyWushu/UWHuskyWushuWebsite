Presented by the Asia Northwest Cultural Education Association, Sakura-Con is the oldest and most well attended anime convention in the Pacific Northwest. Member attendance for Sakura-Con 2013 was over 20,000 individuals, with most members attending all three days! Each year the team is invited to perform alongside local Wushu organizations at the convention and teach attendees during a workshop.

The Chinese Lunar New Year is a very important holiday in Chinese culture -- It is a time to celebrate many different values including good fortune, happiness, wealth, and longevity. Most importantly, it is a time where family comes together. Every year, Chinese Student Association proudly hosts this event free of charge for UW students and the Husky Wushu performs in this important holiday

Each year, a call is made to clubs for the position of host and bids are voted on by the Collegiate Wushu Committee voting members. The CWC is composed of advisors and school representatives, with each school on the CWC posessing one voting representative. The CWC also discusses and votes on changes to tournament rules such as competitor eligiblity requirements and experience level determination. To receive an invitation to the CWC, a school must have hosted at least one Collegiate Wushu Tournament.
